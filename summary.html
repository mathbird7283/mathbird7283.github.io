In this project, I looked at a remarkably specific question -- Would Tampa Bay Rays outfielder Randy Arozarena be a household name in the USA if the COVID-19 pandemic had never happened?

The primary techniques used to answer this question are:

  • Inverse probability weighing, direct standardization, and just a touch of linear regression
  • Analyzing data from recent MLB postseasons
  • A few fairly intuitive assumptions about human behavior
  • Identifying a series of expected values, assuming intervening on both whether the pandemic happened to begin with and whether we bothered to intervene.
  • Identifying monotonicity and Manski bounds, even though further sensitivity analysis was not exactly viable here.

We found that the answer to this question is "probably yes", but our analysis is somewhat incomplete. A limitation is exactly how you measure "household name" in a manner that makes just as much sense in 1991 as in 2022. Additionally, a number of assumptions were made. A possible area of future work may be to find additional unmeasured confounding factors.
